What is the Digital Humanities Research Institute at Southern Methodist University?

The Digital Humanities Research Institute (DHRI) at SMU is a workshop to be held in Summer 2019 at Fondren Library, SMU. DHRI at SMU is still in the planning stages. If you are interested in helping shape this, please contact Rafia Mirza.She can be reached at rmirza@smu.edu

We will be holding an open forum in the Texana room of Fondren Library on September 26th from 11:00am-2:00pm in you are interested in learning more about helping to shape it.

Reasons to Participate

Build Community

Engage with leaders who are passionate about using digital tools to enhance their work.

Learn New Skills

Build technical and professional skills through collaborative and group-oriented workshops

Share Resources

Learn how to teach, adapt, and share our open curricula with others.

What to Expect

The goal for DHRI@SMU is to create an intensive, community-oriented, and foundational approach to learning technical skills in service of humanities teaching and learning.


About Us

GC Digital Initiatives builds and sustains an active community around the shared idea of a "Digital GC," where scholars and technologists explore new modes of inquiry that thoughtfully integrate digital tools and methods into the research, teaching, and service missions of the institution.
